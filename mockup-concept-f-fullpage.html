Decision point: The drawer currently repeats the reuse chip idea inside the slot (filtered to that slot) AND keeps the global reuse strip visible below. Might be redundant — we could hide the bottom reuse strip when a drawer is open, or drop the drawer's reuse section (let the global strip do that job). On the Dress slot being woman-only

In the woman's view (what you see above), the first tile is Dress — tinted slightly pink to feel distinct. In the man's view, that tile disappears and the grid becomes 5 tiles: Top / Layer / Bottom / Shoes / Accessories (or 2×2 with one centered, or we collapse Layer + Accessories into one row). Gender is pulled from userProfile.gender, which you already store. Non-binary / no preference → show Dress by default (it's additive, never blocks).

When a dress is added, the Bottom tile gets dimmed (not hidden) with a subtle "— not needed with dress" label. This way someone who wants leggings under a dress can still tap Bottom and add them; we just don't push it.

On Head merging into Accessories

Good call. Accessories now covers hats, sunglasses, watches, jewelry, belts, bags, scarves, ties. Hats still get their own icon (🧢) in the item list so you can distinguish visually, but they all live in the same slot — which is correct, because they all serve the same mental role: "optional finishing pieces." This also frees the first tile for Dress without making the grid taller.

On the sticky CTA

Sticky bottom nav stays pinned throughout — works the same as today. Gradient fade behind it so card content scrolls underneath without a hard edge. Step label ("Outfit 6 of 10") is centered so you always know where you are in the 10-outfit march.
