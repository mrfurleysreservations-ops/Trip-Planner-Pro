My read: Keep the nav on the bottom (Frame B). Your app is mobile-first, the thumb zone is where the primary tabs belong, and you've already trained your users there. The unlock is the ⋯ More tile: it gives you a growth slot for Flights, Docs, Weather, Gear, Settings — forever — without ever touching the main bar. It also pairs perfectly with your role-density rule: a Planner persona might see Meals in primary and Chat in More; a Guest sees it the other way around. Only swap to top (Frame C) if you start caring about desktop/tablet as first-class surfaces. Until then, top nav just adds another sticky row on mobile without solving anything the sheet doesn't already solve.
